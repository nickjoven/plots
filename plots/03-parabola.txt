PARABOLA  /  BIFURCATION
x² + μ = 0      roots at x = ± sqrt(-μ)

The parabola is the curve y = x² + μ, an upward bowl whose lowest point sits at
height μ on the vertical axis. The roots are the values of x where the curve
meets the x-axis, which are the solutions of x² + μ = 0. Whether those roots
exist depends on the sign of μ, and stepping μ downward past zero is the moment
where the picture changes from no roots to two roots. That change is the
bifurcation.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  lower the bowl by stepping μ down
================================================================

Each frame is the same bowl shifted to a new height μ. As μ steps down, the
bottom of the bowl sinks through the x-axis, and where it cuts the axis the two
roots appear and walk apart.

  μ = +1  (bowl above axis)     μ = 0  (bowl touches)      μ = -4  (bowl cut)
 y                            y                          y
 4 \           /             4 \           /             4 \           /
 2  \         /              2  \         /              2  \         /
 1   \__   __/               0 --\___.___/----- x        0 --*---.---*---- x
 0 ----\_./----- x              (touch at 0)            -2    \_._/   (roots
   no crossing                                          -4      *      -2,+2)
        μ=+1                         μ=0                     μ=-4

roots by frame:
  μ = +1 :  none            the bowl clears the axis
  μ =  0 :  x = 0 (double)  the bowl is tangent to the axis at one point
  μ = -1 :  x = -1, +1      the bowl cuts the axis at two points
  μ = -4 :  x = -2, +2      lowering μ further pushes the two roots apart

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the two roots opening from one
================================================================

The arrival is best seen by plotting the roots themselves against μ rather than
the bowl. Below zero the two roots are plus and minus the square root of minus μ,
so as μ decreases they trace a sideways parabola opening to the left from the
single point at μ = 0.

        x
   +2 |              *  (μ=-4)
   +1 |        *  (μ=-1)
    0 |---*--------------- μ   (μ=0: the two roots meet at one point)
   -1 |        *
   -2 |              *
       0   -1   -2   -3   -4   (μ decreasing to the right)

The single touching point at μ = 0 splits into a matched pair that grows wider as
μ falls. To redraw it by hand, plot the bowl for a few heights and mark where it
crosses the axis, then plot those crossings against μ to see the one point become
two.
