THE SCALE LADDERS
R = 6 x 13^54 = 8.533 x 10^60        Lambda l_P^2 = 13^-108 / 12

Two of the largest numbers in the framework are reached by climbing a ladder
whose every rung is a single factor of thirteen. The ratio R between the Planck
scale and the Hubble scale starts at six and multiplies by thirteen fifty-four
times. The cosmological constant in Planck units starts at one and divides by
thirteen one hundred eight times, then divides by twelve. The ladders are too
long to draw to their last rung, so the rule and the first rungs are drawn and
the rung count is written out.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  one factor of 13 per rung
================================================================

Climbing R from its base of six, each rung multiplies the value below it by
thirteen.

  rung 0 :  6
  rung 1 :  6 x 13          = 78
  rung 2 :  6 x 13^2        = 1014
  rung 3 :  6 x 13^3        = 13182
   ...        ...
  rung 54:  6 x 13^54       = 8.533 x 10^60     (the top)

The ladder for the cosmological constant runs the other way, one hundred eight
rungs downward, each dividing by thirteen, then a final division by twelve.

  Lambda l_P^2 = 13^-108 / 12

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the value at the last rung
================================================================

The arrival of the first ladder is R = 8.533 x 10^60, which sits 0.48 percent
from the observed 8.492 x 10^60 and is sourced to derivation D26. The arrival of
the second ladder is the cosmological constant in Planck units, sourced to
derivation D27. The one hundred eight rungs of the second ladder are twice the
fifty-four of the first, which is the double exponent from item 10. To redraw the
ladders by hand, write the base, then mark each rung as one multiplication or
division by thirteen, and note the rung count rather than reaching the end.
