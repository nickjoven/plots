THE GROUP  SL(2,Z) = <T, S>
T = [[1,1],[0,1]]  (lean)      S = [[0,-1],[1,0]]  (turn)

SL(2,Z) is the set of all two-by-two matrices with whole-number entries and
determinant one. Every such matrix is a move that sends the integer lattice onto
itself while keeping area unchanged, and every one of them can be spelled as a
sequence of the lean T and the turn S. This drawing shows that the lattice of
points never changes under these moves, and that the only thing that moves is the
basis, which is the pair of vectors chosen to measure the grid. Each basis spans
a cell, and every cell has area exactly one.

================================================================
WHOLE view  -  the same lattice, different bases
================================================================

The dots are the same lattice in every frame. The marked pair (b1, b2) is the
basis, and the shaded corner is the cell it spans. The cell changes shape from
frame to frame but always covers one unit of area.

  identity                 after T (lean)           after S (turn)
y                        y                        y

0 O--b1-·  ·             0 O--b1-·  ·             0 b2-O  ·  ·    (b2 at (-1,0))
  O  1  2                    O  1  2                 -1 O  1

bases and cells:
  identity : b1 = (1,0)  b2 = (0,1)    det = 1
  after T  : b1 = (1,0)  b2 = (1,1)    det = 1   the cell leaned
  after S  : b1 = (0,1)  b2 = (-1,0)   det = 1   the cell turned
  after ST : b1 = (0,1)  b2 = (-1,1)   det = 1   leaned then turned

Every basis in the list spans a cell of area one, and the dots they sit on are
the identical lattice in all four frames. Changing the basis re-measures the same
grid; it does not make a new one.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  area one, the lattice fixed
================================================================

The fact that holds across the whole group is that the determinant is one, which
is the statement that area is preserved and that the lattice maps onto itself
with nothing added or lost. The two moves T and S are enough to reach every
element, so the entire group is built from just the lean and the turn. This
area-one condition is the same one that makes the mediant of item 04 the simplest
fraction between two others, and it is the structure the Farey sequence of item
09 is built inside.

To redraw this by hand, plot the lattice once, then draw a few different bases on
top of it. Check that each basis cell covers one grid square of area, and notice
that the underlying dots are unchanged no matter which basis is drawn.
