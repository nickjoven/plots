UNCERTAINTY  tau x delta-theta = 1
the product is fixed at one

The uncertainty relation says that two quantities, here written tau and
delta-theta, have a product fixed at one, so that making one smaller forces the
other larger. It is drawn as the curve xy = 1, a hyperbola, with x standing for
tau and y for delta-theta. Every point on the curve sits at the corner of a
rectangle whose area is exactly one, and sliding along the curve trades width for
height while that area never changes. This is the same area-one condition that
runs through the group in item 07.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  slide along the curve, area stays one
================================================================

The curve passes through the points where the two coordinates multiply to one.
The rectangle drawn from the origin to each point covers one unit of area.

   |          ''---*___           (4, 1/4)

   0    1    2    3    4

  at (1,1)   rectangle 1 by 1     area 1
  at (2,1/2) rectangle 2 by 1/2   area 1
  at (4,1/4) rectangle 4 by 1/4   area 1

As the point slides right, tau grows and delta-theta shrinks by the matching
factor, so the rectangle changes shape but keeps area one.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the fixed product
================================================================

The arrival is the constant product one, the value the area holds at every point
on the curve. There is no single resting point, because the trade can sit
anywhere along the hyperbola; the fixed thing is the product, not the place. The
manifest gives the product as exactly 1.000000 and sources it to derivations D7
and D9. The stretch that moves a point along this curve is the same diagonal
factor a from the Iwasawa decomposition in item 08, which multiplies one
coordinate and divides the other. To redraw it by hand, plot a few points whose
coordinates multiply to one, join them into the curve, and check that each
rectangle under a point covers one square.
