BORN RULE  exponent = 2
probability = amplitude squared

The Born rule says that a probability is the square of an amplitude, and the
number that connects them is the exponent two. It is drawn by taking an amplitude
as a length along the grid and building the square on that length, whose area is
the probability. The step from a side to the area it encloses is the squaring,
and the exponent two is what that step always costs.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  a length becomes its square
================================================================

A length L is laid along the bottom of the grid, and the square on it is filled
in. For a side of three units the area is nine unit cells.

  L = 3 (amplitude)            square on L (probability)
                               3 #  #  #
  o---o---o---o                2 #  #  #
  0   1   2   3                1 #  #  #
                                 1  2  3   area = 3 x 3 = 9

The side is the amplitude and the filled area is the probability. Doubling the
side to six would not double the area but quadruple it to thirty-six, because the
area grows as the side raised to the power two.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the exponent
================================================================

The arrival is the exponent two itself, the fixed power in the relation between
side and area. It does not depend on which length is chosen, since every square
has area equal to its side raised to the second power. The manifest gives this as
exact and sources it to derivations D1 and D9. To redraw it by hand, draw any
length, build the square on it, and count that the area is the side multiplied by
itself.
