SPATIAL DIMENSION  =  3
the dimension ladder

The spatial dimension is three, and it is drawn as a ladder that adds one
independent direction at each rung. The first rung is a point, which has no
direction and is zero-dimensional. The second is a segment, which adds one
direction along its length. The third is a square, which adds a second direction
across the first. The fourth is a cube, which adds a third direction out of the
square. The third spatial direction is the arrival, and it is the same dimension
that turns the integers into the cubes of item 11.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  add one perpendicular direction per rung
================================================================

Each rung takes the figure before it and sweeps it along a new direction that is
perpendicular to all the directions already there.

  dim 0       dim 1            dim 2              dim 3
  point       segment          square             cube
                                                    ____
   *          o---o            o---o              /__/ |
                               |   |              |  | /
                               o---o              |__|/

  0 directions  1 direction    2 directions       3 directions

The point has nothing to sweep, the segment is the point swept along one line,
the square is the segment swept sideways, and the cube is the square swept
upward. Each rung is one sweep, and each sweep adds exactly one direction.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the third direction
================================================================

The arrival is the third rung, the cube, the figure that holds three
perpendicular directions. The framework stops here because space has three
directions, and this is the dimension that makes the side-two and side-three
cubes of item 11 have volume rather than area. The manifest gives the value as
exactly three and sources it to derivation D14. To redraw the ladder by hand,
draw a point, sweep it into a segment, sweep that into a square, and sweep that
into a cube, counting one new direction at each step.
