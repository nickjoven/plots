FAREY SEQUENCE AND THE STERN-BROCOT TREE
|F_6| = 13

The Farey sequence at level n is the list of all fractions between 0 and 1 whose
denominator is at most n, written in increasing order. It is built from the
mediant of item 04: starting from the two ends 0/1 and 1/1, the mediant of each
neighbouring pair is inserted whenever its denominator is no larger than the
current level. Each level therefore adds the new fractions that the mediant
reaches at that denominator. The number of fractions at level six is thirteen,
which is the integer the framework writes as |F_6| and uses throughout the
manifest.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  insert mediants, level by level
================================================================

Each row is one level. A caret marks a fraction newly inserted at that level,
and the count at the right is the number of fractions in the row. The horizontal
spacing is only an aid; the listed order is the truth.

  F_1   0/1 ----------------------------------- 1/1                 count 2
  F_2   0/1 ----------------- 1/2 ------------- 1/1                 count 3
                              ^
  F_3   0/1 ------ 1/3 ----- 1/2 ----- 2/3 ----- 1/1                count 5
                   ^                    ^
  F_4   0/1 - 1/4 -1/3 ---- 1/2 ---- 2/3- 3/4 - 1/1                 count 7
              ^                            ^
  F_5   0/1 1/5 1/4 1/3 2/5 1/2 3/5 2/3 3/4 4/5 1/1                 count 11
            ^         ^       ^           ^
  F_6   0/1 1/6 1/5 1/4 1/3 2/5 1/2 3/5 2/3 3/4 4/5 5/6 1/1         count 13
            ^                                       ^

The counts run 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13. Level six adds only 1/6 and 5/6, because the
other mediants at denominator six are not in lowest terms and were already placed
at earlier levels. The arrival of the process is the count thirteen.

================================================================
THE TREE  -  every fraction as a path of left and right
================================================================

The same fractions form the Stern-Brocot tree. The root is 1/1, sitting between
the ends 0/1 and 1/0, and each node below is the mediant of the nearest fraction
to its left and the nearest to its right among its ancestors. The left half of
the tree holds the fractions between 0 and 1.

                 1/1
                /
             1/2
            /     \
         1/3       2/3
        /   \     /    \
     1/4    2/5  3/5    3/4

Reading the path down from 1/2, a step to the left child or the right child spells
any fraction in the interval as a sequence of L and R moves. This is the path
language that item 12 uses to reach the dark-energy fraction 13/19.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the count 13 and the area-one adjacency
================================================================

The arrival is the count |F_6| = 13. The reason the mediant construction never
skips a fraction is the same area-one fact from item 07: any two neighbours a/b
and c/d in a Farey row satisfy bc - ad = 1, so the vectors (b,a) and (d,c) span a
lattice parallelogram of area exactly one with no point inside. For example 1/3
and 1/2 give 3 times 1 minus 1 times 2, which is 1.

To redraw this by hand, draw the line from 0 to 1, mark 0/1 and 1/1, and keep
inserting the mediant of each neighbouring pair as long as its denominator stays
within the level. At level six there are thirteen marks.
