MEDIANT / MERGE

text and redraw spec
MEDIANT  /  MERGE
a/b combined with c/d  ->  (a+c)/(b+d)      example: 0/1 and 1/1 -> 1/2

The mediant combines two fractions into the single fraction whose numerator is
the sum of the two numerators and whose denominator is the sum of the two
denominators. It is drawn by reading each fraction p/q as the lattice vector
(q,p), so the denominator is the horizontal part and the numerator is the
vertical part. Adding the two vectors tip to tail gives a third vector, and that
third vector points at the mediant. For the example, 0/1 is the vector (1,0) and
1/1 is the vector (1,1), and their sum is (2,1), which is the fraction 1/2.

================================================================
PROCESS  -  add the two vectors
================================================================

The two starting vectors are drawn from the origin. The mediant is their sum,
reached by sliding the second vector so its tail sits at the first one's tip and
marking where it ends.

vectors:
  v1 = (1,0)   the fraction 0/1   (slope 0)
  v2 = (1,1)   the fraction 1/1   (slope 1)
  v1 + v2 = (2,1)   the mediant 1/2   (slope 1/2, between 0 and 1)

The mediant vector (2,1) lies in direction between v1 and v2, and its slope 1/2
sits between the slopes 0 and 1 of the two it came from. The merge is a single
step: one vector addition.

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the simplest fraction between, and why
================================================================

The two starting vectors span a parallelogram, and its area is the determinant
of the pair, which for (1,0) and (1,1) is one. A lattice parallelogram of area
one holds no lattice point inside it and none on its edges except its corners,
so there is no fraction with a smaller denominator sitting between the two. The
mediant is therefore the simplest fraction that fits between them, and this is
the reason it is the value the framework chooses.

  the area-one parallelogram (no point inside):
  1 ·   *-------*      corners (0,0) (1,0) (2,1) (1,1)
        |      /       area = det[(1,0),(1,1)] = 1
  0 ·   *-----*--- x   the mediant (2,1) is one corner

Repeating the merge on each new neighbouring pair is how the Farey sequence and
the Stern-Brocot tree of item 09 are built. To redraw this by hand, plot the two
fractions as vectors, add them, and check that the parallelogram they span covers
exactly one grid square of area.
