THE CUBES AND THE DUTY CYCLE
q2^3 = 8   q3^3 = 27   sum = 35      in dimension d = 3

In dimension three the two base integers become cubes. The integer q2 = 2 becomes
the block two by two by two, whose volume is eight. The integer q3 = 3 becomes the
block three by three by three, whose volume is twenty-seven. Their sum is
thirty-five. The cubing is because the dimension is three, the value drawn in item
16; in a different dimension the same construction would raise the integers to a
different power. The bare reference values the manifest calls the K=1 identities
are all ratios of these two volumes.

The volume is the count of unit cells in the block: eight for the side-two cube
and twenty-seven for the side-three cube. Their sum, eight plus twenty-seven, is
thirty-five.

================================================================
THE DUTY-CYCLE RATIOS
================================================================

Each identity is a comparison of these two volumes, drawn as the ratio of one
block to another or of one block to the combined thirty-five.

  sin^2 theta_W  =  q2^3 / (q2^3 + q3^3)  =  8 / 35   =  0.22857...
  alpha_s/alpha_2 =  q3^3 / q2^3          =  27 / 8   =  3.375
  1/alpha_em (tree) =  q2^3 + q3^3        =  35
  m_H / v        =  1 / q2                =  1 / 2    =  0.5
  lambda_higgs   =  1 / q2^3             =  1 / 8    =  0.125

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  bare values, with their status
================================================================

The arrival is the set of exact ratios above. The manifest records these as
substrate-side reference values at the K=1 point, not as predictions at the Z
scale, and each carries a gap of about one to three percent to the measured value
that the framework declines to close from the grid alone. The drawing therefore
shows them as exact volume ratios and notes that status rather than presenting
them as final physical numbers.

To redraw the set by hand, draw the side-two and side-three cubes, count their
cells as eight and twenty-seven, and form the five ratios above from those two
counts and their sum.
