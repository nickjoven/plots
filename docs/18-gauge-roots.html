THE GAUGE GROUP AS ROOT LATTICES

THE GAUGE GROUP AS ROOT LATTICES
SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1)

The gauge group of the standard model is drawn as three root pictures set side by
side. A root is a direction in which a symmetry can be rotated, and the roots of
each factor form a fixed pattern on the grid. The factor SU(2) is tied to the
integer q2 = 2 and the factor SU(3) to q3 = 3, the same two integers that run
through the whole framework. The arrival is the three patterns together, which is
the gauge content of the standard model shown as lattices rather than as names.

================================================================
SU(2)  -  the root system A1
================================================================

SU(2) has two roots, equal and opposite, lying on a single line. This is the root
system called A1, and it is the pattern tied to q2 = 2.

        -alpha        +alpha
   *------o------*       roots at -1 and +1 on one axis
  -1      0     +1

================================================================
SU(3)  -  the root system A2
================================================================

SU(3) has six roots arranged as a regular hexagon on the triangular lattice. This
is the root system called A2, and it is the pattern tied to q3 = 3.

            *
           / \
       *  /   \  *        six roots at the corners of a hexagon,
        \/     \/         sixty degrees apart, on the triangular grid
        /\     /\
       *  \   /  *
           \ /
            *

================================================================
U(1)  -  a single axis
================================================================

U(1) has no roots; it is a single direction of rotation, drawn as one axis.

   <------o------>        one direction, no root pattern

================================================================
ARRIVAL  -  the three patterns together
================================================================

The arrival is the three root pictures placed side by side: the line of A1, the
hexagon of A2, and the single axis of U(1). The manifest sources the gauge group
to derivations D41 and D42. To redraw it by hand, mark two opposite points on a
line for SU(2), a hexagon of six points on the triangular grid for SU(3), and one
axis for U(1).
